About Me

Life Moto: Love, Serve, Grow

Brian Hansen is from Coeur d'Alene, Idaho, and currently studying finance at Brigham Young University (BYU).

In high school, he took advantage of Idaho's duel enrollment program through North Idaho College and graduated with an Associates of Arts and his high school diploma at age 19. While in high school, he competed in varsity cheerleading and varsity polevaulting while acheiving the goal of 100 community service hours a year. He also was an active member of the National Honors Society, Key Club, and Kiwanis Club. As side projects and hobbies, he started a high school a cappella group named "Take Note" and a community club called "Birdy Roundnet" centered on hosting local Spikeball tournaments and pickups.

Before studying at BYU, he lived in Argentina for two years on an ecclesiastical service mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints where he polished leadership skills by inspiring individuals and communities to stretch to higher levels of their potential. He became fluent in spanish and a lover of mate, an Argentine cultural drink.

Upon returning home Brian rekindled his love for Spikeball, ranking 112th nationally in Spikeball as a semi-professional athlete. For the love of the sport and its community, he serves as the president of the Provo City Spikeball Club.

Outside of sports, Brian is an active member of the a cappella group Secret Chord and volunteers weekly at the Provo City Food Bank and as a temple worker at the Provo City Temple. Brian enjoys writing music, singing, and playing the guitar, and his favorite food is a juicy hamburger.
